Does Being More Inclusive Lead to Worse Graduation Outcomes? — NIRF 2025 (UG 4-Year)

Each institution type is classified by how well it meets the legal reservation quotas (EWS ≥ 10% & SC+ST+OBC ≥ 49.5%). Outcomes are then compared across compliance tiers.
Key metric: Placed + Higher Education % — counts all students with a positive transition (job or further study), because research-oriented institutions route students into postgraduate programmes, not direct employment.

Finding: Inclusion does not harm student outcomes.

Institutions that fully meet both EWS and SC+ST+OBC quotas graduate 90% of students and achieve a combined Placed+HE rate of 91% — virtually identical to those below the quotas. The graduation rate is above 85% in every tier. There is no penalty for being more inclusive.
