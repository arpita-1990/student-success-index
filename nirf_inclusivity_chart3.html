Chart 3 — Who Is Actually Meeting the Quotas? EWS & SC+ST+OBC Compliance Gap · NIRF 2025

Each bar shows how far an institution type is above (green → right) or below (red → left) the legal reservation benchmark. Centre line (zero) = exactly at quota. Bars for UG 3-Year and UG 4-Year programmes are shown side by side.
Key insight: UG 4-Year public institutions mostly meet the SC+ST+OBC quota; UG 3-Year and private institutions frequently fall short.

Reading this chart:

A bar extending right of zero = institution is more inclusive than the quota requires — good. A bar extending left of zero = institution is below the legal minimum — compliance gap. The further left, the larger the shortfall.

✅ Strong EWS compliance Central Minority Univ far exceeds the EWS quota in both UG3 and UG4, driven by minority institution structures. NIT, IIT, State Univ and Private Unaided all comfortably meet the 10% EWS threshold in UG4.
❌ EWS compliance gaps Medical Inst (Public) consistently falls the furthest below the EWS benchmark in both UG3 and UG4. AIIMS and IIT (UG3 only) also fall below — likely due to merit-only or small-batch admission constraints.

✅ Strong SC+ST+OBC compliance Most public institution types (IIT, NIT, AIIMS, Central Univ, State Univ) meet or closely approach the 49.5% threshold in UG4. This is the result of the constitutional reservation system working as intended. IIT improves significantly from UG3 (−20.8 gap) to UG4 (+0.1 gap).
❌ Large SC+ST+OBC gaps — private sector Private Minority (−38.4 pp gap in UG4) and Private Unaided (−28.1 pp gap in UG4) are furthest from the benchmark. Central Minority Univ also falls short because its minority status exempts different student pools. These gaps are structural, not performance-related.
SC+ST+OBC quota benchmark = 49.5% of enrolled students  |  Gap = Actual SC+ST+OBC% − 49.5%  |  Private institutions are legally exempt from reservations — their gap is shown for transparency, not as a violation
